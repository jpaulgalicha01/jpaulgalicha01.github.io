Creative Vegetarian Cuisine


Since opening its doors, Cafe Flora has been at the forefront of utilizing local, organic and sustainable produce and building strong, direct relationships with Washington farms. Its culinary team draws inspiration from international traditions and the availability of abundant, locally grown produce to create imaginative and dynamic vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free cuisine.


Cafe Flora has also carried its eco-conscious philosophy into its sibling restaurants, Floret and the Flora Bakehouse. Floret opened in 2018 and has served as a beacon in the SeaTac airport, offering scratch made, seasonal vegetarian cuisine in a place with nothing else like it. A charming and refreshing respite in the bustling airport, Floret offers travelers a chance to take a breath whether they have 3 minutes or 30.


The newest addition to the Flora family, Flora Bakehouse, is now open! Offering a cozy cafe space where freshly baked pastries and bread can be enjoyed alongside a delicious cup of coffee.


The winning combination of eco-friendly philosophy, community based involvement, and cutting-edge, dynamic vegetarian cuisine has brought about tremendous support for all three locations. Come by and enjoy a bite!

Meet the Owner


Nat Stratton-Clarke is the owner of the Flora Restaurant Group: Cafe Flora, The Flora Bakehouse, and Floret. He has fostered a haven for vegetarians and vegans alike for almost 30 years with local, organic, and sustainable goods with an eye on being eco-conscious and producing food that is simply delicious. As a transgender business owner, he strives to cultivate a safe, respectful, and diverse environment for guests and his 100+ employees as well as contributing to organizations such as Camp Ten Trees and GLSEN to help support their ongoing missions. He and his wife, Erin, reside in Madison Valley with their 3-year old twin daughters.
